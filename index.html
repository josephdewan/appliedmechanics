Past Research And Experience

Spring of 2023

Designing and prototyping a new a pendulum system to simulate an impact that produces 200g of force onto a mannequin head. I will have to create a wall mounting plate that is completely rigid. The pendulum itself will be contructed using t-slotted frames.

Fall of 2022

Designing a new helmet design to reduce the forces that are absorbed by the head during a large impact. The proposed plan is to connect the helmet to the body so that a larger massed object can prevent the head from rotating and creating long term spinal and neck damage. A centripetal locking mechanism will be used to allow the head to rotate freely when small accelerations occur, but lock completely to the body when higher accelerations are introduced like during a large impact.

Summer of 2022

The goal of this research is to obtain 3D scans that 1) contain enough detail to calculate distances and normal vectors, 2) have a sub-millimeter accuracy, and 3) are easily reproducible to the average consumer. This included LiDAR and photogrammetry scanning techniques. Using these methods, we can evaluate accuracies related to helmet manufacturing and stretching due to daily wear.
